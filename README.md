## Business Impact:

Analysing Wikipedia page views across a range of date helps in making strategic business decision based on public curiosity. Wikipedia page traffic represents intentional search rather than passive media consumption, it serves as a direct representation of market interest and demand.

This project demonstrates the use of public data to gain competitive advantage. By using specific timeframe, businesses can leverage web traffic patterns instead of reactive decision making. 

Web traffic data can be used to:

1.Anticipate demand shifts: Catch trends and consumer behaviour before they saturate the market.

2.Validate investments: Quantify impact of marketing strategies and product launches using unbiased third party metric.

3.Optimise operations: Risk management and supply chain forecasting based on real time public attention spikes.

## Results: 

Measuring the "shelf-life" of a cultural moment or news event. A steep vertical spike with a rapid drop indicates viral hype (like a trending Netflix show : Wednesday).

The relationship between the show and the actress in seen to be proportional. Page view for Jenna Ortega seems to follow similar trend as that of "Wednesday".

A gradual rise with a slow decay indicates a sticky, shifting macro-trend (like changing public sentiment on an individual : Cristiano Ronaldo).

The popularity of the player seemed to spike during the FIFA world cup and followed a gradual spike and decay, showing the public interest in the sports during that timeframe.

Popularity of individual like Maria Telkes seem to spike on 12th December, and then decay immediately afterwards.
